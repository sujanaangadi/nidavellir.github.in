PHASE 1 : CITY MAP — Nidavellir

Southwestern Rajasthan (Harji – Posalia – Jawal – Sumerpur region)

Location

Southwestern Rajasthan — between Harji, Posalia, Jawal, and Sumerpur near the Aravalli Hills and Jawai River.

Terrain

Hilly and rocky near the Aravalli range; flat fertile plains near the Jawai basin — ideal for farming and settlement.

Soil

  • Sandy-loam in plains — supports bajra, wheat, and pulses
  • Rocky soil in hill areas

Rivers

Jawai and Sukri — major water sources for agriculture and drinking.

Climate

Semi-arid climate with hot summers (up to 44°C), mild winters (10–25°C), and moderate monsoon (400–600 mm rainfall).

Vegetation

Dry deciduous and thorn forests — Khejri, Babool, Neem, and Ber trees commonly found.

Natural Resources

  • Granite, marble, limestone, sandstone
  • Medicinal plants and forest produce

Wildlife

Close to Jawai Leopard Hills Sanctuary — rich biodiversity, crocodiles, migratory birds, hyenas, and eco-tourism value.

Topography

Mixed landscape of hills, valleys, and river plains — allows natural drainage and scenic city development.

Suitability for City Development

  • Reliable water supply from Jawai River
  • Stable land for housing & industries
  • Strong potential for eco-friendly tourism and renewable energy
